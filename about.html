    Early Interests

    I've always been interested in computers. I did some basic coding in high school such as HTML and lego robotics. I had so many interests at the time, and the school did not offer opportunities to build on the skills I learned. In college, I was exhausted from studying AP Biology and did not want to pursue a degree in a science-related field even though I was always fascinated by technology and science. I loved and double majored in History and Government.

    Sparks

    I did some internships for NYC elected officials and decided that I would prefer to work for the NYC government than at other levels because I thought I could have the most positive and direct impact because the issues are at the local level. Within a year I started working for a NYC agency, I was inspired by my senior supervisor to build my own computer. He built his and provided some advice. I renewed my interst in computers, especially about hardware since I did some tinkering as a child when I installed a graphics card and cleaned some fans. I thought about studying to get a COMPTIA+ certificate and start a career as a IT Technician in the IT Department, but I was not interested enough to find myself committed to the studies. Around this time, I started my Masters in Public Administration.

    Fireworks

    What really set off the fireworks was when I got into building circuits with kits and scratch from Adafruit. I tried to make a Gps Step Tracker for my parents' dog but the code did not work. It was in C, so I started to teach myself C from Harvard's CS50 on EDX.org. Never finished because of my studies and jumped over to MIT's Introductory class in Python. Some day, I'll finish that GPS step tracker!

    Capstone

    At some point halfway through my master's program, I got a new position within my agency with increased responsibilities. My agency released a new system called DYCD Connect, and I became very interested in governments' websites and database systems. My capstone was about the people's experience using government websites and compare that to non profit and the private sector. I learned that I wanted to be more involved in the government's work to better its technological systems so that the quality of services are improved for the people.

    OSU

    I decided to enroll in OSU Computer Science program to assist me in developing required skills needed to achieve my career transition. I can bring my experience working as a Program Manager who experienced at the front level to understand what the systems should be doing and why to make it more friendly for everyone.

    Data

    Then, with DYCD Connect, I became really interested in Data Science/Analytics. I developed questions, ran queries and reports, analyzed the data, created a data visualization on PowerPoint, and presented to an audience up to forty people.
